Meet the Team

We currently have 8 ferry pilots available; primarily based around the UK and Western Europe. All have a minimum of 1500 hours and have all operated in a professional capacity while flying light aircraft for surveying, glider towing, instructing, ferry flying and enjoy flying for fun. We do not utilise low hour pilots and all have experience of independently planning, coordinating and operating ferry flights. Collectively our roster of pilots has flown over 40 aircraft types and over 30,000 hours.


Mike Osborne


Mike has been flying for over 10 years, with a background of varied experience working within the Aviation industry including corporate flight operations. He has flown the Jetstream 41 and Dash 8 commercially for several years, as well as flying as a tug pilot for his gliding club. He has experience flying light aircraft across Europe and Africa, additionally to this he enjoys flying microlights and motor gliders. Mike is a well-rounded and experienced pilot in both commercial and general aviation, he takes pride in completing his operations in a safe and efficient manner.

Mike Hadley


Mike has been flying for over 10 years, with 6 years experience working in the aviation industry. Initially beginning as an Instructor for his local Volunteer Gliding Squadron, Mike then went on to gain experience in aircraft dispatch before settling in a dynamic operations department. During this time, he coordinated the ferry operations of a large flight school around Europe and the Middle East. Mike spent 3 years flying the Boeing 737 professionally around Europe and Northern Africa. Additionally, he has enjoyed flying SEP & MEP aircraft around Europe on many occasions. As well as across the US and Canada. In founding M², he is now looking forward to taking a new step, utilising his repertoire of experience to build an exciting and reputable company.

Ash Basnett


Ash joins us with over 12 years flying experience, bringing with him a wealth of single engine & multi engine piston and single engine turbine hours. He has several thousand hours in single engine piston aircraft across Europe, has operated the PC12 into some of the most challenging airports in Europe (looking at you Courchevel!) and has taught several of the UK RAF’s Red Arrows display pilots. Ash maintains a keen enthusiasm for flying and flight safety, he is a multi-engine instrument instructor and examiner.

Luke Pickup


Luke joins us with over 6 years flying experience, he has a wide range of flying experience flying the Let410, Jetstream 41, ERJ and Boeing 747 commercially. He is currently operating as a Jetstream 41 Captain and has extensive ferry flight experience in these categories of aircraft.
